The following is an outline of the desired features of this app once it
has reached its final stage.

The app is designed to have the following features:

- A study timer which logs study hours. Hours spent in study is a
useful metric because it allows one to set measurable study goals.

- Manual input for study hours. Many gamified study apps do not have
a manual tool to input study hours if not using the study timer,
leading to a potential loss of data.

- A subject-based "formula sheet". As content is added under a subject 
to the app, a formula sheet is compiled with content to practice.
Content could be proofs, formulas, theses, vocabulary, or definitions.

- The SM-2 study repetition algorithm. It is important to expand the
intervals between practicing content to ensure its long-term memory.
The SM-2 algorithm is a rudimentary way of organizing these intervals.


An outline of the architecture of the app is as follows:

- Sheet Page:
  - Colored side-tabs on the left where each is linked to a subject.
  - Clicking on a side-tab displays a different subject sheet in the
center.
  - Each subject sheet is a formula sheet which includes all the
content you have entered into the app.
  - Each entry in the formula sheet is colored in accordance with its
difficulty to you.
  - Two top tabs which allow you to toggle between your formula sheet
and practice problems.

- Pomodoro Page:
  - A clock that you start/stop which auto logs.
  - A click-through window when you start the clock to rate energy
levels.
  - A click through window when you end to: 
    - rate the activity in terms of ease from 0-5 for the SM-2 
      algorithm;
    - describe what the activity was (proofs > practice problems/
      project > notecards > note taking > light reading);
    - select the items that were practiced on the formula sheet;
    - add items to the formula sheet and practice sheet.
  - A button to manually enter/edit times spent studying in the system
database.

- Daily Page:
  - Displays content for the user to practice according to the SM-2 
algorithm. The priority increases with the number of days passed the 
suggested practice date, unless dismissed.
  - Graphy of daily study time w/ colored blocks corresponding to
difficulty.
  - Itensity factor for each block calculated using the SM-2 difficulty
multiplied by some fraction related to the difficulty of the specific 
task.
